# Template content safety

System templates are documentation aids, not medical decision systems. Their text
uses editable headings for history, assessment, plan, and follow-up without
preloaded diagnoses, medications, doses, prescriptions, automated orders, or
universal clinical targets.

The psychiatric risk template supports professional documentation only. It does not
calculate a score, diagnose, or replace emergency procedures; immediate risk must
follow the local clinical protocol. Pediatric, obstetric, cardiology, endocrinology,
and other specialty templates similarly avoid automatic interpretations or treatment
recommendations.

Consent templates are base formats only and visibly require medical, administrative,
and legal review before use. Procedure fields such as `[Nombre del procedimiento]`
remain literal text. They do not assert legal sufficiency for any jurisdiction.

Future specialty and legal review should occur before publishing a new catalog
version. Existing clinic copies, clinical notes, and consent snapshots are not
modified by catalog updates.
